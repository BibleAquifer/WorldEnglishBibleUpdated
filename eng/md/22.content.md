## Song of Songs 1:1 (id: 1822001001)

1The Song of songs, which is Solomon’s.


## Song of Songs 1:2 (id: 1822001002)

2Let him kiss me with the kisses of his mouth; for your love is better than wine.


## Song of Songs 1:3 (id: 1822001003)

3Your oils have a pleasing fragrance. Your name is oil poured out, therefore the virgins love you.


## Song of Songs 1:4 (id: 1822001004)

4Take me away with you. Let’s hurry. The king has brought me into his rooms. We will be glad and rejoice in you. We will praise your love more than wine! They are right to love you.


## Song of Songs 1:5 (id: 1822001005)

5I am dark, but lovely, you daughters of Jerusalem, like Kedar’s tents, like Solomon’s curtains.


## Song of Songs 1:6 (id: 1822001006)

6Don’t stare at me because I am dark, because the sun has scorched me. My mother’s sons were angry with me. They made me keeper of the vineyards. I haven’t kept my own vineyard.


## Song of Songs 1:7 (id: 1822001007)

7Tell me, you whom my soul loves, where you graze your flock, where you rest them at noon; for why should I be as one who is veiled beside the flocks of your companions?


## Song of Songs 1:8 (id: 1822001008)

8If you don’t know, most beautiful among women, follow the tracks of the sheep. Graze your young goats beside the shepherds’ tents.


## Song of Songs 1:9 (id: 1822001009)

9I have compared you, my love, to a steed in Pharaoh’s chariots.


## Song of Songs 1:10 (id: 1822001010)

10Your cheeks are beautiful with earrings, your neck with strings of jewels.


## Song of Songs 1:11 (id: 1822001011)

11We will make you earrings of gold, with studs of silver.


## Song of Songs 1:12 (id: 1822001012)

12While the king sat at his table, my perfume spread its fragrance.


## Song of Songs 1:13 (id: 1822001013)

13My beloved is to me a sachet of myrrh, that lies between my breasts.


## Song of Songs 1:14 (id: 1822001014)

14My beloved is to me a cluster of henna blossoms from the vineyards of En Gedi.


## Song of Songs 1:15 (id: 1822001015)

15Behold, you are beautiful, my love. Behold, you are beautiful. Your eyes are like doves.


## Song of Songs 1:16 (id: 1822001016)

16Behold, you are beautiful, my beloved, yes, pleasant; and our couch is verdant.


## Song of Songs 1:17 (id: 1822001017)

17The beams of our house are cedars. Our rafters are firs.


## Song of Songs 2:1 (id: 1822002001)

1I am a rose of Sharon, a lily of the valleys.


## Song of Songs 2:2 (id: 1822002002)

2As a lily among thorns, so is my love among the daughters.


## Song of Songs 2:3 (id: 1822002003)

3As the apple tree among the trees of the wood, so is my beloved among the sons. I sat down under his shadow with great delight, his fruit was sweet to my taste.


## Song of Songs 2:4 (id: 1822002004)

4He brought me to the banquet hall. His banner over me is love.


## Song of Songs 2:5 (id: 1822002005)

5Strengthen me with raisins, refresh me with apples; for I am faint with love.


## Song of Songs 2:6 (id: 1822002006)

6His left hand is under my head. His right hand embraces me.


## Song of Songs 2:7 (id: 1822002007)

7I adjure you, daughters of Jerusalem, by the roes, or by the hinds of the field, that you not stir up, nor awaken love, until it so desires.


## Song of Songs 2:8 (id: 1822002008)

8The voice of my beloved! Behold, he comes, leaping on the mountains, skipping on the hills.


## Song of Songs 2:9 (id: 1822002009)

9My beloved is like a roe or a young deer. Behold, he stands behind our wall! He looks in at the windows. He glances through the lattice.


## Song of Songs 2:10 (id: 1822002010)

10My beloved spoke, and said to me, “Rise up, my love, my beautiful one, and come away.


## Song of Songs 2:11 (id: 1822002011)

11For behold, the winter is past. The rain is over and gone.


## Song of Songs 2:12 (id: 1822002012)

12The flowers appear on the earth. The time of the singing has come, and the voice of the turtledove is heard in our land.


## Song of Songs 2:13 (id: 1822002013)

13The fig tree ripens her green figs. The vines are in blossom. They give out their fragrance. Arise, my love, my beautiful one, and come away.”


## Song of Songs 2:14 (id: 1822002014)

14My dove in the clefts of the rock, in the hiding places of the mountainside, let me see your face. Let me hear your voice; for your voice is sweet and your face is lovely.


## Song of Songs 2:15 (id: 1822002015)

15Catch for us the foxes, the little foxes that plunder the vineyards; for our vineyards are in blossom.


## Song of Songs 2:16 (id: 1822002016)

16My beloved is mine, and I am his. He browses among the lilies.


## Song of Songs 2:17 (id: 1822002017)

17Until the day is cool, and the shadows flee away, turn, my beloved, and be like a roe or a young deer on the mountains of Bether.

## Song of Songs 3:10 (id: 1822003010)

## Song of Songs 4:14 (id: 1822004014)

## Song of Songs 4:15 (id: 1822004015)

## Song of Songs 4:16 (id: 1822004016)

## Song of Songs 5:1 (id: 1822005001)

1I have come into my garden, my sister, my bride. I have gathered my myrrh with my spice; I have eaten my honeycomb with my honey; I have drunk my wine with my milk. Eat, friends! Drink, yes, drink abundantly, beloved.


## Song of Songs 5:2 (id: 1822005002)

2I was asleep, but my heart was awake. It is the voice of my beloved who knocks: “Open to me, my sister, my love, my dove, my undefiled; for my head is filled with dew, and my hair with the dampness of the night.”


## Song of Songs 5:3 (id: 1822005003)

3I have taken off my robe. Indeed, must I put it on? I have washed my feet. Indeed, must I soil them?


## Song of Songs 5:4 (id: 1822005004)

4My beloved thrust his hand in through the latch opening. My heart pounded for him.


## Song of Songs 5:5 (id: 1822005005)

5I rose up to open for my beloved. My hands dripped with myrrh, my fingers with liquid myrrh, on the handles of the lock.


## Song of Songs 5:6 (id: 1822005006)

6I opened to my beloved; but my beloved left, and had gone away. My heart went out when he spoke. I looked for him, but I didn’t find him. I called him, but he didn’t answer.


## Song of Songs 5:7 (id: 1822005007)

7The watchmen who go about the city found me. They beat me. They bruised me. The keepers of the walls took my cloak away from me.


## Song of Songs 5:8 (id: 1822005008)

## Song of Songs 5:9 (id: 1822005009)

## Song of Songs 5:10 (id: 1822005010)

10My beloved is white and ruddy. The best among ten thousand.


## Song of Songs 5:11 (id: 1822005011)

11His head is like the purest gold. His hair is bushy, black as a raven.


## Song of Songs 5:12 (id: 1822005012)

12His eyes are like doves beside the water brooks, washed with milk, mounted like jewels.


## Song of Songs 5:13 (id: 1822005013)

13His cheeks are like a bed of spices with towers of perfumes. His lips are like lilies, dropping liquid myrrh.


## Song of Songs 5:14 (id: 1822005014)

14His hands are like rings of gold set with beryl. His body is like ivory work overlaid with sapphires.


## Song of Songs 5:15 (id: 1822005015)

## Song of Songs 5:16 (id: 1822005016)
